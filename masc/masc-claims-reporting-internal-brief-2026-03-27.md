# MASC Internal Brief
## How to File a Claim / How to Report

**Purpose**
Align internally on the problem, client preference, and delivery constraints before proposing `2` to `3` viable directions back to MASC.

**Problem Statement**
The current `How to File a Claim` and `How to Report` pages feel too long and scroll-heavy. Users have to move through a large amount of content before finding the specific program or process they need, which weakens clarity, wayfinding, and task completion.

**Current UX Problem**
- Too much content is stacked on a single page.
- Users do not have a fast way to jump to the specific insurance program they need.
- The pages currently combine multiple programs and reporting/claim processes into one long reading flow.
- Example concern raised: there are roughly `5` different insurance programs and related process instructions being surfaced in one place.

**Client Preference**
- MASC is leaning toward a `parent / child` breakout.
- Current preference is for `How to Report` to become a parent page.
- Individual programs would become child pages.
- The parent page would use a product-card style pattern to route users to the child pages.
- Both `How to File a Claim` and `How to Report` should follow the same structural pattern.

**Scope / Constraints**
- This is new scope, not just cleanup of the current implementation.
- The team should treat this as custom feature work.
- Design and content both need to be part of the internal decision.
- MASC would like to see this before the `April 15, 2026` launch.
- The team does not need to finalize implementation in this meeting, but it does need to align on direction and effort.

**Meeting Goal**
- Confirm the problem in a way everyone agrees on.
- Align on the preferred content and UX pattern.
- Leave with one recommended direction and `2` to `3` solution candidates suitable to send back to MASC.
- Align on effort, priority, and whether the preferred direction is realistic before launch.

**Discussion Prompts**
- What is the simplest pattern that reduces scrolling and improves findability?
- Does the parent / child model solve the problem cleanly enough to justify the added build scope?
- If both pages share one pattern, what should stay consistent across them?
- What can realistically be designed, built, content-populated, and reviewed before launch?

**Relevant Prior Meeting Context**
- Danielle outlined two broad directions: a more complex secondary-navigation approach that layers content, and a less complex parent-page pattern using the existing `Insurance Overview` icon/button treatment.
- Wes agreed to bring the problem back internally, assess implications, and provide cost direction for the preferred path.
